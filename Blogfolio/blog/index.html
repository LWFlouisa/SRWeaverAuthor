2024 10 30 Emulsion Paints: Differences From Sumi Ink

What is Sumi Ink

Sumi Ink is a type of ink used for Japanese brush painting, with the ink using either charcoal or lamp soot as the pigment. And mixed with animal hyde glue.

While my type of paint has simularities, there are also some key differences.

What To Expect

My types of paint I use act more like a hybrid of Sumi Ink, and Gouache Paint. With the key difference being that I use an emulsion. It's flow is reminiscent of alcohol inks, but also dries like oil paint.

Recipe

1 tablespoon Rubbing Alcohol or Vodka
1 tablespoon Linseed or Walnut Oil
1 tablespoon of Rice Vinegar
1 tablespoon Charcoal, Graphite, or ground spices
1 tablespoon of Calcium Carbonote
1 teaspoon of honey or glycerin
1 teaspoon of baking soda ( optional )

Carefully mix vodka, linseed oil, and rice vinegar as an emulsion. Then add one teaspoon of honey.

Slowly add small aounts of pigment, especially if scaling the batch to ten recipes. Make sure its evenly mixed. Apply Calcium Carbonate in the same method.

Also experimenting with adding yeast to ferment it.

( Optional ) Add 1 teaspoon of baking soda to change its PH value

Paper Used

This can be used with either Sumi Paper, or Western Watercolor Paper. You should stick with Gansai paints for manga paper.

Words Of Caution

Remember to use pigments that don't contain lead or cadmium. Natural Earth Pigments is ideal for this use case.
