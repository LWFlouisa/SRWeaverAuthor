What is Oil Gouache Ink

Oil gouache ink combine Oil paint, gouache, and india ink into a cohesive inking system:

Oil Paint - a paste made with ground pigment and a drying oil such as linseed oil, used chiefly by artists. Gouache - A mixture of pigment, filtered water, honey, chalk, and gum arabic. India Ink - A thin water solution that blends activated charcoal or graphite, with vinegar as a mordant, and alcohol as a solvent.

Oil Gouache Ink - A thin solution made by dissolving pigment in alcohol and honey to disperse the pigment and mixing it with vinegar linseed or walnut oil. Chalk is then added to make it more opaque. Gum arabic can be added to prevent seperation of the emulsion.

Basic Recipe

1/2 Cup of Pigment, Charcoal, or Graphite
1/4 Cup of Chalk
1/4 Cup of rubbing alcohol
1 TBSP of honey
1/4 Cup of rice vinegar
1/2 Cup of Linseed or Walnut Oil
1 TBSP gum arabic

First dissolve and mix the pigment in 1/4 cup of Rubbing Alcohol, until it becomes a thick paste. Add 1 tablespoon of honey. Evenly mix.

Add 1/4 Cup of rice vinegar, and shake till it becomes a pigmented mix of alcohol and vinegar.

Now add 1/2 Cup of linseed or walnut oil and 1/4 cup of chalk. Shake until it becomes an emulsion. Make sure the bottle is large enough, as this will bubble.

Once it's finished bubbling, finally add gum arabic powder to bind the emulsion together.

Differences From Standard Gouache

This can be used for many of the same purposes as traditional gouache, but can also be used for the same canvases you would do Oil Painting.

The type of Oil you use will depend on whether you're painting on Cotton ( use Linseed Oil ), or wood ( use Walnut Oil ).

Also this cannot be reactivated with water, but can be thinned with Rice Vinegar.

Differences From Oil Paint

Because of this mixture you don't have to thin it with Turpentine to use it. And remains thin like ink if maintained.

Oil Sticks

By substituting Gum Arabic for Gum Tragacanth you can also make these into Oil Sticks, with accelerated drying abilities.

Recommended Brushes

I personally use Sumi Brushes rather than Kolinsky brushes for linework, and kolinsky brushes for detail work. Oil Sticks are for signing your name.
